Privacy Policy for Routines

Effective date: 17 July 2026

Routines (application ID software.fel.routines) is an Android habit tracker whose main surface is a home-screen widget: one row per routine, tap a row to mark it done for today. This policy explains how the app handles your data. The short version: it doesn't collect any.

Data we collect

None. The app collects no personal data, sends nothing to any server, and contains no analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind. The released app requests no runtime permissions and no internet access permission, so it is technically incapable of transmitting your data off the device.

What you create

You create your routines inside the app: their names, which ones show on the widget and in what order, and — each time you tap a routine — a record that it was done, timestamped in your device's local time. The app does not read your files, folders, contacts, location, or any other data on your device.

Data storage

Everything the app tracks — your routines and their completion history — is stored locally on your device as a single JSON entry in the app's private storage (an Android SharedPreferences file shared between the app and its widget). This data never leaves the device and is removed when you uninstall the app.

Third parties

The app uses no third-party services, SDKs, or servers. No data is shared with anyone.

Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children and collects no data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date.
